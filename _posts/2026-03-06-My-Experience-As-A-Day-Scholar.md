For context I am an engineering student , currently in 4th year and I lived in home during these 4 years . 

It’s not like I chose not going to hostel . My college happened to be in my city and 30 -45 mins from my home and of course I don’t hate my family . 

Being is hostel is definitly fun ofc (as I have heard) but staying at home isn’t that bad as well . I got to enjoy the luxury of food prepared and clothes washed for 4 more years 

and during these 4 years a lot of people have told me about how I missed the fun in life because I stayed at home , and it does make me wonder have I ?

I do agree that hostels are fun , you learn adulting , you live with your friends all the time , you have fun .

But staying at home ,being a homebody, wasn’t a bad experience for me at all . I loved being able to have AC while the hosteller practically lived in Libraries the whole summers ,or being able to take long hot showers in winters ,or having good warm food and the luxury of not doing laundry . 

I won’t say that these are the prime needs but it is a luxury I get to enjoy a little longer. 

I got to grow up , enter my 20s in front of my parents , an age where my parents could actual talk to me about stuff they were worried about because they thought of me as a responsible adult . I got to stay at home and with my parents a little longer before I become a traveller in my city ( the pain poets and writer talks about all the time) . Being a Day scholar allowed me semi adulting , a stage where I have to deal with my own problems but I still get to run away to the comfort of home anytime I want to retire for the day . 

I being a day scholar missed out on the fun and freedom hostels provide but the hostellers also missed the warmth of home , the tea time with parents or the dinner table discussions . 

I am just starting my life and I will eventually end up being away from home , in a hostel or PG someday, but the people who moves out at 18 do they get to return ? do they get that 4 years of comfort again? 

Then again they didn’t choose being away just like I didn’t choose staying in .
