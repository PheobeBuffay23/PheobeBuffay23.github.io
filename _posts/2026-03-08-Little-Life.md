A Little Life is written by Hanya Yanagihara. It is about 700+ pages long.

There were two reasons I picked up this book.
First, I wanted to read something soul-shattering (I know that’s not exactly the healthiest thing to want). I hate to admit it, but I saw a lot of Instagram posts and reels about this book where people were bawling their eyes out while reading it. Were they really being honest about it?
Second, I haven’t read anything longer than 300 pages yet, so this book is my longest read so far. I didn’t want to miss out on good books just because they are 500–700 pages long.
Well, that’s something that still needed to be discovered.
I have read almost 60% of the book and right now I have mixed feelings about it. I have been reading this book on and off for about three months and I’m still reading it, so that means it isn’t boring. But I do admit that I have skipped a few — or maybe more than a few — pages in between.
The story is primarily about Jude (the main character), and we see different stages of his life as he grows older.
What I like about this book:
This book doesn’t revolve only around Jude. Even though he is the main protagonist, the story also shows that every character has their own life and their own burdens.
What I don’t like:
I am actually a bit pissed off by the amount of time I have spent on this book. It started off well, but later it felt like the author simply had a lot of words and wanted all of them to be in the book.
I don’t understand why people on Instagram were bawling their eyes out over this book. I was definitely looking for a soul-crushing story, but instead it mostly crushed my brain.
I’m no therapist, but I don’t think that’s how grief works.
I also don’t understand the author’s obsession with sexual explanations. I believe more than 40% of the book talks about sexual trauma, desires, and the inability to deal with them.However, I eventually pushed through and finished the book, even though it took me quite a long time.
Well, the good thing is that I completed a 700-page book for the first time. The bad thing is that I spent more or less five months on it.
